Cybersecurity Q&A

Q: What is a firewall?

A: A firewall is a network security device that monitors and filters incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predetermined security rules. It acts as a barrier between trusted internal networks and untrusted external networks, such as the Internet.

Q: What is two-factor authentication (2FA)?

A: Two-factor authentication (2FA) is a security process that requires users to provide two different authentication factors to verify their identity. This typically involves something you know (like a password) and something you have (like a mobile device or security token).

Q: What is a VPN?

A: A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is a service that allows you to create a secure, encrypted connection to another network over the Internet. It masks your IP address and encrypts your online activity, enhancing your privacy and security, especially when using public Wi-Fi networks.
